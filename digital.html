Being, Over Time explores life, aging, emotions, and relationships as ongoing cycles that move from physical existence to emotional and spiritual experience. Beginning with the body and everyday emotional states, the work follows how these conditions change through interaction with others.

Using simplified systems and scientific measures such as heart rate, arousal, and valence, the project reflects both change and unchange across growth, emotion, and repetition.

Using chlorophyll as a conceptual entry point, the project explores vitality, growth, and life cycles through the tree as a living system. By simulating shifts in color and energy over time, it reflects how life persists through repetition, transformation, and renewal.
